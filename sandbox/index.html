Advice for Leaders on the World Stage
Advice for people working with those leaders
United States
Recognize that others may not share your action orientation

Don’t let your positivity come across as naive optimism

Build your capacity to listen
Get to the point, be brief, and focus on tangibles

Recognize that Americans value efficiency and action

Be straightforward and open
Middle East
Adopt a less hierarchical, more inclusive leadership style

Display comfort with ambiguity

Don’t be proud or defensive in response to feedback
Invest in building trust and long-term relationships

Be sensitive to the tension between tradition and modernity
India
Resist interpreting your role too narrowly

Think “team first”

Develop ideas inclusively

Be less process-bound
Discourage individualism

Give feedback sensitively and be patient with resistance

Encourage initiative and questioning
Latin America
Don’t feel rejected when others behave formally

Adopt a less directive style

Be ready to give and receive honest feedback
Build warm relationships

Ensure that structures and processes are tight

Deliver tough feedback in a sensitive and positive manner
Europe
Be more action-oriented

Be more flexible in response to change or uncertainty

Operate in a less structured and rigid manner
Respect people’s desire for inclusion

Focus on alignment and buy-in

Devote more time to execution

Honor roles and decision rights
China
Stay positive when facing setbacks

Be less cautious in airing views and taking action

Balance analysis with conceptual exploration
Play into the drive to improve

Use insiders to help build relationships

Appreciate the importance of harmony and balance
Sub-Saharan Africa
Build deep relationships and close teamwork

Recognize the value of structure and organization

Work to win hearts and minds
Break down barriers by being less formal; use humor

Recognize the value of clarity

Invest time in developing people and teams
